ACRYLIC ON CANVAS

The Journey

Born in the vibrant heart of Caracas, Venezuela, Verónica Carrasquel's path to the canvas was not paved with traditional academic blueprints. She is a self-taught force, driven by an innate necessity to translate the kinetic energy of her surroundings into static, yet breathing, forms.

Her migration to Buenos Aires marked a pivotal shift in her visual language. The architectural geometry of the southern metropolis began to merge with her ancestral tropical rhythm, leading her to the precise world of optical art. It was here that her exploration of dualism found its definitive voice.

Technique

For Carrasquel, acrylic on canvas is not just a medium, but a meticulous discipline. Her technique demands an almost surgical precision, where 1mm of deviation can break the optical illusion. She works in a state of meditative focus, layering high-contrast pigments to create depth that defies the two-dimensional surface.

Philosophy

"The canvas is the bridge between light and shadow. I don't paint shapes; I paint the silence between them."

Her artistic philosophy revolves around the concept of 'Equilibrium through Tension.' By utilizing the sharp contrast of black and white, she challenges the viewer's perception, forcing the eye to find harmony in a space of constant movement. It is a reflection of the dualities we inhabit—chaos and order, presence and absence.

format_quote

"My work is a rhythmic dialogue between the eye and the spirit. It requires stillness to be truly seen."

Artistic Principles

The Binary Lens

By stripping away color, Verónica focuses the narrative on pure composition. The binary relationship of black and white represents the fundamental forces of the universe.

Kinetic Stillness

Her work creates the illusion of movement on a static surface, mirroring the constant flow of life despite our attempts to capture moments in time.

The Caracas Influence

The brutalist architecture and chaotic beauty of her hometown provide the structural DNA for her minimalist explorations in Buenos Aires.

Infinite Depth

Each piece is an invitation to look deeper, uncovering hidden patterns that only emerge through prolonged observation and focus.
